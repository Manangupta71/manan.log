Why Have a Blog?

July 11, 2026 ⏱ 4 min read

No Substack, no blue-purple UI, no algorithm. Just HTML, opinions, and a missed fellowship deadline that finally made me ship this thing.

Good question. I'd say — why not?

First off, I don't like X. As someone who gets ragebaited easily, I do NOT want to have the urge to punch someone through the screen at 3am. Reddit is better, but I have some opinions that won't neatly fit into a subreddit.

All the portfolio sites I've seen recently are, in the nicest way possible, deprived of human touch — which is ironic, considering they ought to be a human's representation. If I see the blue-purple UI one more time, I will think about smashing my laptop. (I can't actually smash it, though, because then where will I rant from?)

I wanted to build something of my own, hence no Substack. The idea was born quite a while ago, and like many things, I procrastinated it a lot — until one day the research internship I wanted to apply to (hint: ABC) asked for a personal site, and my first instinct was: hmm, the forms will stay open for a while, I'll do this later.

Ladies and gentlemen, the saxophones just got louder — the form closed earlier than anticipated, and I completely missed out on the opportunity. So when another fellowship required a personal project, I decided to finally ship this one out. No blue-purple UIs, just HTML and opinions (and also a lot of coffee, since I have lately turned into a night owl).

The Old Style

The old style is inspired by one of the sites I spent most of my middle school years on — it's called Quotev. You could read fanfics on there, take quizzes to find out if you'd survive the Creepypasta killers, and chat with other users. It was like Wattpad and Buzzfeed had a book-loving, edgy kid.

The website is still around, though it's not as popular as it used to be. Still, it's one of my favorite cozy internet corners.

An Act of Resistance

I also like to think of this site as an act of resistance. It's simple, it's fun, and it's something I'll actually update. Also, I think it's really niche.

In a tech world obsessed with productivity, optimisation, and efficiency — a world I am also an active participant in, as a CS undergrad — I love the idea of having something so simple and personal it defies all the rules.

I have a lot of ideas for this site. Who knows — maybe this will evolve into the paulgraham.com of its time. (Read: that would mean I evolve into the Paul Graham of my time. I am not sure if that's my path yet.)

But dear reader — stay, enjoy, and if you want, drop me an email to say hi.
